Acetyl-CoA

Combines with Oxaloacetate to begin the Citric Acid Cycle.

Citrate

Enzyme: Citrate Synthase

Reaction:
Oxaloacetate + Acetyl-CoA → Citrate

Type:
Condensation Reaction

Importance:
First irreversible step of TCA cycle.

Isocitrate

Enzyme: Aconitase

Reaction:
Citrate ↔ Isocitrate

Type:
Isomerization

Importance:
Rearranges hydroxyl group for oxidation.

α-Ketoglutarate

Enzyme: Isocitrate Dehydrogenase

Products:
NADH + CO₂

Type:
Oxidative Decarboxylation

Importance:
Major regulatory step.

Succinyl-CoA

Enzyme: α-Ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase

Products:
NADH + CO₂

Importance:
High-energy thioester compound.

Succinate

Enzyme: Succinyl-CoA Synthetase

Products:
GTP formed

Type:
Substrate-level phosphorylation.

Fumarate

Enzyme: Succinate Dehydrogenase

Products:
FADH₂ formed

Location:
Inner mitochondrial membrane.

Malate

Enzyme: Fumarase

Reaction:
Fumarate + H₂O → Malate

Type:
Hydration reaction.

Oxaloacetate

Enzyme: Malate Dehydrogenase

Products:
NADH formed

Importance:
Regenerates Oxaloacetate for next cycle.

Location
